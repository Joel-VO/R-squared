THIS CRIMINAL MISC. CASE HAVING COME UP FOR ADMISSION ON
09.01.2019, THE COURT ON THE SAME DAY PASSED THE
FOLLOWING:

ORDER

Petition filed under Section 482 Cr.P.C.

2. Petitioners are the accused in CC No. 4516 of
2012 on the file of the Judicial First Class
Magistrate Court, Irinjalakkuda. They are
alleged to have committed the offences under
sections 7 and 8 of the Kerala Gaming Act (for
short, “the Act”). The proceedings are sought
to be quashed on the ground that the
prosecution has no case that the incident took
place in a common gaming house.

3. Heard the learned counsel for the petitioners
and the learned Public Prosecutor.

4. It is not in dispute that to attract sections
7 and 8 of the Act the incident should take

place in a common gaming house. In this case
 2019%KERINOSS

crt.mc.to. 191 of 2019

the prosecution has no such allegation. It
follows that the proceedings against the
petitioners are liable to be quashed.

In the result, this Crl.M.c is allowed. The
proceedings in CC No. 4516 of 2012 on the file
of the Judicial First Class Magistrate Court,
Trinjalakkuda are quashed. —_Rs.1,00,000/-
(Rupees One lakh only) out of the money seized
from the petitioners shall be credited to the
Chief Minister's Distress Relief Fund and the
balance amount shall be refunded to the

petitioners.
