This case having come up for consideration before this Court on
09.01.2025 and this Court on the same day passed the following

ORDER

1. This is a case wherein Charge Sheet was filed by the Sub Inspector of
Police, Sreekaryam Police Station in Crime No. 1718/2021 against the accused
alleging commission of offences punishable under _—_Sections
498A,,506,325,352,354 r/w 34 of the Indian Penal Code,1860 and 75 of Juvenile
 Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 (hereinafter referred to as
the ‘JJ Act’),

2. The prosecution case is that though the Informant and the Accused
04.09.2007 and have two children in the wedlock. Accused has been harassing
his wife and the children physically and mentally, after demanding dowry and
that the Informant lost her-sight partially and caused a burn injury on the leg of
the eldest child. Thereby the Accused are alleged to have committed the above-
mentioned offences.

3. The case was originally taken on file as C.C. No. 122/22 and cognizance
was taken in respect of the said offences. Thereafter, processes were issued to
the Accused Persons. On their appearance, copies of relevant, Prosecution
records were furnished to them, The Accused are already enlarged on bail. The
case was posted for framing of charge, at which juncture it was brought to the
attention of this Court that CWs 2 and 3, the children were also injured and
offences alleged includes offence under Section 75 of the JJ Act.

4. This Court considered the submissions of both the Learned APP and the
Learned Counsel for the Accused as to the competency of this Court to try the
above offences as the same involved violation of child rights and also offence
punishable under Section 75 of the JJ Act. The Learned APP also pointed out that
the settled position in the light of Section 86(4) of the JJ Act read in consonance
with Section 25 of the Commission for Protection of Child Rights Act that in
cases involving violation of child rights, this Court is not competent to conduct
the trial and the Court competent to try the same is the Hon’ble Children’s
Court, ie., the Hon’ble Principal District & Sessions Court, Thiruvanathapuram.
Thus, on satisfaction of the above submission and legal position, the said case
was refiled as C.P. No. 28/2024 for the purpose of committing the above case to
the Hon'ble Children's Court for trial.

4. In the light of the above circumstances, this case is committed under
Section 232 of the Bhartiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhitha (hereinafter referred to
as the ‘BNSS') to the Hon'ble Principal District and Sessions Court,
Thiruvananthapuram for trial.
 5. The Accused Persons shall appear before the Hon’ble Principal District and
Sessions Court, Thiruvananthapuram as and when required. The entire case
records including the documents and material objects, if any, shall be forwarded
to the Hon'ble Court aforesaid at the earliest.

6. The Learned APP shall be notified as to the committal of the above case

and the office is directed to retain copies of all relevant records prior to the same
being sent to the Hon‘ble Court.

{is not competent to conduct the trial and the Court competent to try the same is the
