LUDGMENT
1. Accused stands trial for offences punishable under sections 447, 294(b), 323,

and 324 r/w.34 of Indian Penal Code, 1860

2, The prosecution case in brief is as follows := On 21.11.2012,, at 9.30 pm, at
Nirmalalayam Convent Thekkekunnu road Manvila, Kulathoor Ward, Attipra
Village, the accused no.1 by using a wooden piece struck on the head of CW1,
resulting in injury. Thus the accused Nos. 1 and 2 thereby committed offenses

‘w/ss. 447, 294(b), 323 and 324 r/w.34 of Indian Penal Code, 1860

3. Final report was filed by the Sub Inspector of police, Thumba police station
before the Judicial First Class Magistrate Court-Il, Thiruvananthapuram and
the case was taken on file as C.C. 489/2013. Cognizance was taken for the

offences punishable w/s. 447, 294(b), 323 and 324 r/w.34 of Indian Penal Code,
 306

1860 against the accused persons. Thereafter, it was transferred to this court, in
pursuance of the order of the Hon'ble Chief Judicial Magistrate,

‘Thiruvananthapuram, the case was refiled as CC. 353/2018.

4. On appearance of the accused Nos. 1 and 2 they were enlarged on bail on
23.11.2012. Copies of all relevant prosecution records were furnished to them
under section 207 Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. After hearing
prosecution and counsel for accused Nos. 1 and 2, charge framed for offences
ws, 447, 294(b), 323 and 324 r/w.34 of Indian Penal Code, 1860, read over
and explained to the accused Nos. 1 and 2 to which they pleaded not guilty and

claimed to be tried.

5. On the side of prosecution, PW1 was examined and Exhibit P1 was marked.
‘As the material witnesses failed to support the prosecution case, the learned
Assistant Public Prosecutor gave up the CW2 to CW7. In the absence of any
incriminating circumstances against the accused Nos. 1 and 2, questioning of
the accused Nos. 1 and 2, under section 313(1)(b) Code of Criminal Procedure,
1973 was dispensed with. Accused Nos. 1 and 2 were called upon to adduce
evidence but no defence evidence was adduced from the side of the accused

Nos. 1 and 2.

10. The learned Assistant Public Prosecutor had given up other witnesses and he is

justified in doing so as the material witness did not support the case of

prosecution. Having considered evidence on record, this court is of the view

the said reason these points are found against the prosecution.
