ORDER

This is a proceedings under Section 482 of the Code of
Criminal Procedure for quashing Annexure - A2 Final Report
pending trial before the Judicial First Class Magistrate Court,
Kalamassery in C.C.No.1914 of 2016.

2. The petitioner is the accused in the said case.
The case was one registered under Sections 406 and 420 read
with Section 34 of the Indian Penal Code.

3. It is seen that the petitioner and the de facto
complainant have amicably settled the disputes. An affidavit
sworn to by the de facto complainant to that effect is part of the
records.

4. Heard the leamed counsel for the petitioner,
the learned Public Prosecutor as also the learned counsel for

the de facto complainant.
 Mc. 2858 of 20: Fy 2020KERISO

5. In the light of the decision of the Apex Court in
Gian singh v. State of Punjab, (2012) 10 SCC 303, insofar as
the offences alleged against the petitioner are only offences
punishable under Sections 406 and 420 read with Section 34 of
the Indian Penal Code, | am of the view that this is an
appropriate case where this court has to invoke the jurisdiction
under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure to quash
the proceedings.

In the result, the CriM.C. is allowed and Annexure -
A2 Final Report pending before the Judicial First Class
Magistrate Court, Kalamassery in C.C.No.1914 of 2016 is

quashed.
