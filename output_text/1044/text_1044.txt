This case having been finally heard on today the court on the same day delivered the
following :~

JUDGMENT

1, Accused stands trial for offences punishable under sections 457 and 380 of

Indian Penal Code, 1860.

2. The_prosecution_case_in_brief_is_as_follows ;- Accused with dishonest

intention committed theft of gas cylinder belongs to CW1 Angel Mary on
08.10.2012. ‘Thus the accused has committed offences punishable under

sections 457 and 380 of Indian Penal Code, 1860.

3. Final report was filed by the Sub Inspector of police, Valiyathura police station
before the Hon'ble Chief Judicial Magistrate Court, Thiruvananthapuram and
the case was taken on file as C.C. 236/2012. Cognizance was taken for the
offences punishable u/ss. 457 and 380 of Indian Penal Code, 1860 against the

accused. Thereafter, it was transferred to this court, in pursuance of the order
 30S

of the Hon'ble Chief Judicial Magistrate, Thiruvananthapuram, the case was
refiled as CC. 788/2015. The accused was absconded. ‘The case was then
transferred to the register of long pending cases as LP. 19/2019. On
18.03.2020, accused surrendered before this court and the case was refiled as

CC.209/2020, which is the present case.

4. On appearance of the accused, he was enlarged on bail on 18.03.2020. Copies
of all relevant prosecution records were furnished to him under section 207
Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. After hearing prosecution and counsel for
accused charge framed for offences u/s. 457 and 380 of Indian Penal Code,
1860 read over and explained to the accused to which he pleaded not guilty and

claimed to be tried.

5. On the side of prosecution, PW1 was examined. As the material witnesses
failed to support the prosecution case, the learned Assistant Public Prosecutor
gave up the CW3 to CW6. CW2 is reported no more. Death certificate
produced, verified and accepted. In the absence of any incriminating
Circumstances against the accused questioning of the accused under section
313(1)(b) Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 was dispensed with. Accused
‘was called upon to adduce evidence but no defence evidence was adduced

from the side of the accused.

6. Heard both sides and perued records.
 405

1, Whether, on 08.10.2012 the accused committed house breaking with an
intention to commit theft of the gas cylinder and thereby committed

offence punishable under section 457 of Indian Penal Code, 1860?
2. Whether on the same day, at same time accused committed theft of a

gas cylinder and thereby accused committed an offence punishable u/s.
380 of Indian Penal Code, 1860?

3 If the accused is found guilty? What is the order as to sentence?

8. Boint Nos. 1 and 2 :-To avoid repetition and for brevity, point numbers 1 and
2 are considered together. Prosecution case is that accused has committed

offences punishable under sections 457 and 380 of Indian Penal Code, 1860.

9. PW1 deposed that she has no complaints against the accused and she cannot
identify the gas cylinder which was alleged to be stolen. She further deposed

that she has no grievance that the accused has taken the cylinder.

10. The learned Assistant Public Prosecutor had given up other witnesses and he is
justified in doing so as the material witness did not support the case of

prosecution, Ha

1g considered evidence on record, this court is of the view
that prosecution has failed to prove the guilt of accused. For the said reason

these points are found against the prosecution.
 S ofS
11, Point No, 3 := Upon the finding in point nos. 1 and 2, the accused is found not

guilty of offences punishable under sections 457 and 380 of Indian Penal Code,
1860 and he is acquitted for the said offences under section 248(1) Code of
Criminal Procedure, 1973, His bail bond stands cancelled and he is set at
liberty.
