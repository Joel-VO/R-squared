ORDER

Petitioner is the accused in C.C.No.1153/2014 of the
Judicial First Class Magistrate's Court-1, Punalur which has
arisen from Crime No.546/14 of the Yeroor Police Station,
Kollam. The case relates to the offences punishable under
Section 420 of the Indian Penal Code and Sections 3 and
17 of the Kerala Money Lender's Act.

2. The petitioner has come up with this Crl.M.C.
under Section 482 CrRC. for getting Annexure A1 final
report in Crime No.546/2014 of the Yeroor Police Station,
now pending before the Judicial First Class Magistrate's
Court, Punalur as C.C.1153/2014, quashed.

3. The allegation against the petitioner is that the

defacto complainant had borrowed an amount of %3 lakhs

from the petitioner on 05.05.2010 by furnishing signed blank
cheque leaves, signed blank stamp papers etc. as security.
Even though the defacto complainant had paid an amount of
214,40,000)- to the petitioner by way of repayment towards
the said transaction, the petitioner has not cared to return
the documents obtained by him from the defacto
complainant. According to the petitioner, the matter has
been amicably settled between him and the defacto
complainant and presently the defacto complainant has no
complaints against him.

4. Heard learned counsel for the petitioner, the
learned counsel for the defacto complainant, who is the 3°
respondent herein, and the learned Public Prosecutor.

5. The defacto complainant, who is the 3°
respondent herein, has filed an affidavit affirming that the
matter has been amicably settled between her and the
petitioner and, therefore, she does not want to proceed
against the petitioner. As the matter relates to a money

transaction and when the matter has been settled, there is

Station, and all proceedings pending before the Judicial
First Class Magistrate's Court-l, Punalur on it as
