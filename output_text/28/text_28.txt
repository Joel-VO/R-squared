ORDER
Dated this the 22” day of January , 2024
This Cri.M.C., under Section 482 of Cr.P.C., is filed by

the sole accused in Crime No.189 of 2022 of Atholy Police
Station, Kozhikode, registered under Sections 448, 341,
323, 506 and 294(b) of IPC, for quashing all proceedings in
C.C.No.465 of 2022 on the file of Judicial First Class

Magistrate Court-II, Perambra.

2. The prosecution allegation is that on 06.06.2022, the
accused, in a fit of rage, against the 3rd respondent, who
was his ex-wife, trespassed into the house of respondents

3 and 4, and physically assaulted and verbally abused

them.

3. When the matter is taken up for consideration,
learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that the
matter stands settled between himself and respondents 3
and 4. The defacto complainant and 4th respondent

entered appearance through counsel, and submitted that
 224KERISUAS

the matter stands settled. Annexures A3 and A4 are
affidavits filed by them, endorsing the settlement. They
have no intention to proceed with the prosecution, and
they have no objection in quashing the proceedings in

C.C.No.465 of 2022.

4. On instructions, learned Public Prosecutor submitted
that the matter is settled and he produced signed
statement of respondents 3 and 4. In that statements
also, they have stated that they are not intending to
proceed with the prosecution, and they have no objection

in quashing the proceedings in C.C.No.465 of 2022.

5. Since the matter stands settled, no purpose would be

served by continuing the prosecution against the petitioner.

6. _ In the light of the principles laid down by the Apex
Court in Gian Singh v. State of Punjab and Another;
[(2012) 10 SCC 303)], and State of Madhya Pradesh
v. Laxmi Narayan and Others; [(2019) 5 SCC 688],

this Court is of the view that the proceedings against the
 224KERISUAS

petitioner can be quashed on the basis of settlement.
Moreover, the dispute is purely personal in nature and no

Public interest is involved in it.

In the result, this Crl.MC is allowed, and the
proceedings in C.C.No.465 of 2022 on the file of Judicial

First Class Magistrate Court-II, Perambra, stands quashed.
