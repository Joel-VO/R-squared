THIS CRININAL MISC. CASE HAVING COME UP FOR ADMISSION ON
22.01.2024, THE COURT ON THE SAME DAY PASSED THE FOLLOWING:
 224KERASSS

ORDER
Dated this the 22“ day of January , 2024
This Cri.M.C., under Section 482 of CrP.C., is filed by

accused Nos.1 to 3 in Crime No.1490 of 2023 of Kannur
Town Police Station, Kannur, registered under Section 498A

r/w Section 34 of IPC, for quashing Annexure A1 FIR.

2. The prosecution allegation is that, accused Nos.1 to

3, who are the husband and

in-laws of the defacto

complainant/2™ respondent, subjected her to matrimonial

s, demanding more gold and money.

3. When the matter is taken up for consideration,
learned counsel for the petitioners submitted that the
matter stands settled between themselves and the defacto
complainant/2™ respondent. The defacto complainant
entered appearance through counsel, and submitted that
the matter is settled, and Annexure A2 is the affidavit filed

by her, endorsing the settlement. She has no intention to
 224KERASSS

proceed with the prosecution. She has no objection in
quashing the proceedings pending against the petitioners
pursuant to Crime No.1490 of 2023 of Kannur Town Police

Station.

4. On instructions, learned Public Prosecutor submitted
that the matter is settled, and he produced signed
statement of the 2” respondent, to the effect that she is
not intending to proceed with the prosecution, and she has
no objection in quashing Annexure A1 FIR in Crime

No.1490 of 2023 of Kannur Town Police Station.

5. Since the matter stands settled, no purpose would be
served by continuing the prosecution against the
petitioners.

6. In the light of the principles laid down by the Apex

Court in Gian Singh v. State of Punjab and Another;

[(2012) 10 SCC 303)], and State of Madhya Pradesh
v. Laxmi Narayan and Others; [(2019) 5 SCC 688],

this Court is of the view that the proceedings against the
 224KERASSS

petitioners can be quashed on the basis of settlement.
Moreover, the dispute is purely personal in nature, and no

public interest is involved in it.
In the result, this Cri.M.C. is allowed and Annexure
Al FIR in Crime No.1490 of 2023 of Kannur Town Police
Station, stands quashed.
