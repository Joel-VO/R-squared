LUDGMENT
1. Accused stands trial for offences punishable under sections 323, 324, 448,
341, 506(i) and 354 read with section 34 of Indian Penal Code, 1860.

2. The prosecution case in_brief is as follows ;- Due to the prior enmity On
18.3.2023, at 7 am the accused no.1 trespassed into the premises of the house
of CWI and break opened the door of her bathroom and kicked on her right
thigh and thereafter destructed the flush tank .It is alleged that that when CW 1
ran away from there to her house the accused no .1 to 3 were followed her ,and
the accused no.3 caught hold her ,thereafter the accused no.1 fisted on her
back and the accused no.2 slapped on her face . Thus accused Nos. 1 to 3 have
committed offenses u/ss. 323, 324, 448, 341, 506(i) and 354 read with section
34 of Indian Penal Code, 1860.

2. Final report was filed before this court by the Sub Inspector of police Thumba
police station and the case was taken on file as CC. 51/2024. Cognizance was
taken for the offences punishable wiss. 323, 324, 448, 341, 506(i) and 354 read
with section 34 of Indian Penal Code, 1860 against the accused persons.

3. On appearance of the accused Nos. 1 to 3 they were enlarged on bail on
(06.04.2023. Copies of all relevant prosecution records were furnished to them
under section 207 Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. After hearing prosecution
‘and counsel for accused Nos. 1 to 3 charge framed for offences w/ss. 323, 324,
448, 341, 506(i) and 354 read with section 34 of Indian Penal Code, 1860, read
‘over and explained to the accused Nos. 1 to 3 to which they pleaded not guilty
and claimed to be tried.

4, On the side of prosecution, PW1 was examined and Exhibit P1 was marked. As
the material witnesses failed to support the prosecution case, the learned
Assistant Public Prosecutor gave up the CW2 to CWIO.. In the absence of any
incriminating circumstances against the accused Nos.1 to 3 , questioning of the
accused Nos. 1 to 3, under section 313(1)(b) of CPC. was dispensed with.
Accused Nos. 1 to 3 were called upon to adduce evidence but no defence

evidence was adduced from the side of the accused Nos 1 to 3.

5. Heard both sides and perused records.
