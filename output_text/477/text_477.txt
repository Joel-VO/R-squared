ORDER

1.The petitioner is the first accused in C.C.No.1170 of 2013
on the file of the Judicial Magistrate of First Class-I,
Cherthala. He is being proceeded against for having
committed offence punishable under Section 21 of the
Mines and Mineral (Development and Regulation) Act,
1957 and Section 118(e) of the Kerala Police Act read

with Section 34 of the IPC.

2.The learned counsel appearing for the petitioner submits
that by Annexure-A2 judgment in W.P.(C) No.26779 of
2013, the petitioner herein was permitted to file an

application before the respondent and seek for
 2016KERIAA6I

CrILM.C. No, $407 of 2016 2
compounding the offence and the respondent was also
directed to pass orders in compliance with the directions
in Digil v. Sub Inspector of Police [2013(1) KLT 600].
In pursuance to Annexure-A2 judgment, a sum of
Rs.25,000/- was remitted towards compounding fee which
is evidenced by Annexure-A3 and the offence was
compounded. According to the learned counsel, in view
of the decision of this Court in Digil v. Sub Inspector of
Police (supra), when compounding fee has been collected
and the vehicle has been released, no further proceedings
can be taken in respect of the offence under the MMDR

Act.

3.1n view of the above, insofar as the offence under Section
21 of the Mines and Mineral (Development Regulation)
Act, 1957 is concerned, the prosecution cannot be
sustained. However, the criminal proceedings under

Section 118(e) of the Kerala Police Act read with Section
