Petitioner is the 1% acoused in Crime
No.714/2014 of Tirur Police Station for the offences
punishable under Sections 464, 465 and 471 read with
Section 34 IPC and Section 12B of the Passports Act, 1967.
Itis alleged that the petitioner had consented to his brother,
who is the 2" accused, to acquire a passport by using the
name and address of the petitioner.

2. According to the petitioner, his brother had made
the mischief by showing the name and address of the
petitioner for acquiring a passport. Annexure-A2 letter of
the District Police Chief, Malappuram, clearly shows that the
‘enquiry conducted revealed that the brother of the petitioner
had earlier obtained an Indian passport £2658676

impersonating him as the petitioner and performed journey
 (CALM.C.8455/2017

abroad. When the said fact have come to light, the
petitioner cannot be an accused in the crime.

3. The registration of the crime as against the
petitioner is not legally sustainable and all further
proceedings against the petitioner in Crime No.714/2014 of
Tirur Police Station are liable to be quashed.

In the result, this Cr.M.C. is allowed and Annexure-A1
First Information Report in Crime No.714/2014 of Tirur
Police Station and all further proceedings as against the
petitioner in it, are quashed.
