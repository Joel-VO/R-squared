This case having been finally heard today, the court delivered the following:
JUDGMENT

1. Accused stands trial for offences punishable under sections 447 and 427 read
With 34 of Indian Penal Code, 1860.

2. Prosecution case in brief is as follows:- On 03.02.2023, at 9.25 pm, accused
‘nos. 1 to 3 infurtherance of their common intention, trespassed in to the
‘compound of the Unit No.25, Valiyaveli, Poundkadavu Ward, Attipra Village,
the house of CW1 and destroyed the CCTV camera placed on the roof of the
said house by pouring charcol on it and caused loss of Rs. 9000/- to CW1.
‘Thus accused persons have committed offences punishable under sections 447

and 427 read with 34 of Indian Penal Code, 1860.

3. Final report was filed before this court by the Sub Inspector of police Thumba

police station and the case was taken on file as CC. 52/2024. Cognizance was
 3016
taken for the offences punishable under sections 447 and 427 read with 34 of

Indian Penal Code, 1860.

4. On appearance of accused Nos. 1 to 3 they were enlarged on bail on
26.04.2023. On their appearance relevant records of prosecution were served
ws. 207 of Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. Particulars of offences
punishable under sections 447 and 427 read with 34 of Indian Penal Code,
1860 read over and explained to the accused Nos. 1 to 3 to which they pleaded

not guilty and claimed to be tried,

5. On the side of prosecution, PW1 was examined and Exhibit. P1 was marked.
As the material witnesses failed to support the prosecution case, the learned
Assistant Public Prosecutor gave up the CW2 to CW9. In the absence of any
incriminating circumstances against the accused persons questioning of the
accused Nos. 1 to 3, under section 313(1)(b) of Code of Criminal Procedure,
1973 was dispensed with. Accused Nos. 1 to 3 were called upon to adduce
evidence but no defence evidence was adduced from the side of the accused

Nos. 1 t0 3.

6. Heard both sides.

7. Points that arose for consideration are as follow:

1. Whether on 03.02.2023, at 9.25 pm, accused nos.1 to 3 trespassed into Unit
'No.25, Valiyaveli, Poundkadavu Ward, Attipra Village, the house of PW1
‘with their common intention to commit mischief and thereby accused nos. 1
to 3 committed an offence punishable under section 447 r/w. 34 of Indian
Penal Code, 1860 ?

2 Whether on the same time and place infurtherance of their common
intention of accused Nos. 1 to 3, accused no.1 among them poured charcol
on the CCTV camera placed in the said house and caused loss worth Rs.
9,000/- to PW thereby committed offence punishable under section 427

r/w. 34 of Indian Penal Code, 1860?

If the accused Nos. 1 to 3 are found guilty? What is the order as to

sentence?

Point. Nos. 1and 2 :-To avoid repetition and for brevity, points are considered

together, Prosecution case is that the accused Nos. 1 to 4 have committed

offences punishable under sections 447 and 427 read with 34 of Indian Penal

Code, 1860.

PW1 admitted her signature in the Ext. P1. She deposed that she is not aware
whether the loss was caused by accused nos. 1 to 3 and further deposed that

she has no grievance against the accused persons.
 50t6
10. Learned Assistant Public Prosecutor has given up the other witnesses in this
case and he is justified in doing so as the material witness failed to support the
prosecution case, There is nothing on record to disclose that the accused Nos. 1
to 3 have committed the offences as alleged by the prosecution. Having
considered the evidence on record, this court is of the view that the
prosecution has failed to prove the guilt of the accused persons and for the said

reasons, these points are found against the prosecution.

11, Boint No, 3 := Upon the findings in Point Nos. 1 and 2, the accused Nos. 1 to 3
are found not guilty of offences punishable under sections 447 and 427 read
with 34 of Indian Penal Code, 1860 and they are acquitted of the said offences
under section 255(1) Code of Criminal Procedure.1973, Their bail bonds

stands cancelled and they are set at liberty.
