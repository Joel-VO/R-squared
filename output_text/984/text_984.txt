This Petition having finally come up for consideration on 09.01.2025 and this,
Court on 10.01.2025, passed the following:

ORDER

1. Thisis a Petition filed by the Petitioner herein to withdraw his earlier plea and,
toallow him to plead guilty to the offence alleged against him.

2. The Petitioner herein is the sole Accused in the above case facing changes for
the offences punishable under Sections 279, 337, 338 of Indian Penal Code
(hereinafter referred to as ‘the IPC’) and 146 r/w 196 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988
(hereinafter referred to as the ‘MN. Act’.

3. The case of the Petitioner is that at the time when the particulars of the
offences were read over to him, he pleaded not guilty to the offences alleged and

claimed to be tried without properly understanding the contents and consequences
 thereof due to some confusion. Now the case is posted for examination of the
Prosecution witnesses and the Fetitioner intends to plead guilty to the offences
alleged and that he has come up voluntarily after fully understanding the
consequences thereof. Hence this Petition.

4. The Leamed APP objected to the same on the ground that, at this stage, when
wes was partly
xamined-in-chief; and therefore at his juncture the Petitioner cannot resile from his
earlier admission and the same is an abuse of process of law. Per contra, the Learned
‘Counsel for the Petitioner submits that the plea of the Accused is voluntary and he
has clearly submitted the circumstances under which his erstwhile plea has been
recorded and there is no prohibition to record the subsequent plea. He relies on
Rohit Suresh v. State of Kerala, 2023: KER: 60578 to buttress his contention,

‘the case is posted for examination of the witnesses and one of the wi

5. have carefully gone through the materials on record and the considerations
of the Learned Counsel for the Petitioner and the Learned APP. The specific case of
the Petitioner is that he intends to plead guilty to the above offences and that he
pleaded not guilty earlier without properly understanding the contents and
consequences thereof due to some confusion. It is setled that when the guilt is
admitted by the Accused and the admission is found to be voluntary, there is no
reason why the Court should not allow him to withdraw his claim to be tried and
plead guilty. See Suo Motu v State of Kerala, 2023 (2) KH 178, Santhosh v. State of
Kerala, 2003 KHC 135. Applying the principles in the above decisions, lam unable to
accept the contention of the Learned APP. I am satisfied that the Petitioner has
voluntarily come up to plead guilty to the offences alleged and after having been
appraised of the consequences of his pleading guilty thereto. Hence this Petition is
allowed.

6. Inthe result, the Petition is allowed. The Petitioner is allowed to withdraw his
claim to be tried and plead guilty.
