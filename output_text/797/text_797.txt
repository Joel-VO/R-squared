This is a proceedings instituted under Section 482 of
the Code of Criminal Procedure for quashing the proceedings in
C.C.No.243 of 2019 on the files of the Court of the Judicial

Magistrate of First Class - III, Thrissur.

2. The petitioners are the accused in the said case.
The said case was instituted upon a private complaint lodged
by the first respondent under Sections 465, 467, 469 and 471

read with Section 34 of the Indian Penal Code.

3. It is seen that the occurrence referred to above is
one arose on account of the estranged matrimonial relationship

between the first peti

ner and the son of the first respondent.
It is also seen that the petitioners and the first respondent have
settled their disputes, and an affidavit to that effect has been

filed by the first respondent,

4. The learned counsel for the first respondent
affirms that the affidavit is one sworn to by the first respondent

and submits that the first respondent has no objection in

granting the relief sought for by the petitioners.

5. In the light of the decisions of the Apex Court in
Gian singh v. State of Punjab, (2012) 10 SCC 303,
Narinder singh v. State of Punjab, (2014) 6 SCC 466 and
Jitendra Raghuvanshi & Others v. Babita Raghuvanshi &
Another, (2013) 4 SCC 58, | am of the view that this is an
appropriate case where this court has to invoke the jurisdiction
under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure to quash

the proceedings against the petitioners.

In the result, the Cri.M.C. is allowed and the
proceedings in C.C.No.243 of 2019 on the files of the Court of

the Judicial Magistrate of First Class - Ill, Thrissur are quashed.
