JUDGMENT

1. Accused stands trial for offences punishable under sections 323 and 324 of

Indian Penal Code, 1860.

2, The prosecution case in brief is as follows :- Due to the previous enmity, on
24.10.2012, at 19.00 hours, the accused at the road margin of Bypass road, near
Paruthikuzhi Junction, Puthanpalli Ward, Muttathara Village, the accused fisted
con the chest and face of CW/1, when he fell down due to the hit, the accused hit
on his back with a granite piece resulting in injury. Thus accused committed

offences punishable under sections 323 and 324 of Indian Penal Code, 1860,

3. Final report was filed by the Sub Inspector of police, Poonthura police station
before the Judicial First Class Magistrate Court-Il, Thiruvananthapuram and
the case was taken on file as C.C. 1918/2012. Cognizance was taken for the
offences punishable w/ss. 323 and 324 of Indian Penal Code, 1860 against the
accused. Thereafter, it was transferred to this court, in pursuance of the order
of the Hon'ble Chief Judicial Magistrate, Thiruvananthapuram, the case was

refiled as CC. 1638/2018.

4. On appearance of the accused, he was enlarged on bail on 31.10.2012. Copies
of all relevant prosecution records were furnished to him under section 207

Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. After hearing prosecution and counsel for

9. PW1, Mahin deposed that, he had given statement before the police regarding
the alleged incident of this case and he identified his signature in the first
information statement which is marked as Ext. Pl. According to him the

accused was not assaulted him and he has no grievance against the accused.

10. The learned Assistant Public Prosecutor had given up other witnesses and he is
justified in doing so as the material witness did not support the case of
prosecution, Having considered evidence on record, this court is of the view
that prosecution has failed to prove the guilt of accused. For the said reason

these points are found against the prosecution.

11, Point No, 3 := Upon the finding in point nos. 1 and 2 the accused is found not
guilty of offences punishable under sections 323 and 324 of Indian Penal
Code, 1860 and he is acquitted for the said offences under section 248(1) Code
